Match the style of `posts/202510_vitd/blog.qmd` and `posts/202601_health/blog.qmd`:

- **Hook at the top**: open with bold one-liners framing the research question, setting, and result (like "**The challenge:** ... **The method:** ..."), before any `##` heading.
- **Headings**: `##` for all sections, sentence case, concrete and informative (not generic).
- **Bold**: use `**bold**` liberally for key terms, numbers, and concepts on first mention.
- **Concrete numbers**: always state effect sizes explicitly ("6%", "58% within six months").
- **Spacing**: use `\` and `<br>` between sections, consistent with the other posts.
- **Tone**: direct, informative, not over-academic. Punchy sentences. Avoids filler.
- **No trailing summary**: don't end with a generic "conclusion" paragraph restating everything.

**Why:** User flagged that first draft didn't match their established blog style. Reference posts are the canonical examples.

**How to apply:** Before writing any new blog post, read at least one of the reference posts to calibrate tone and structure.
